---
title: "Risk detection"
description: "How Kairo automatically identifies and flags at-risk Coders."
---

Kairo continuously analyses each Coder's academic progress and soft skill scores to detect when someone may need support. When the risk engine identifies a concern, it creates a **risk flag** that your TL dashboard surfaces immediately.

## How risk level is calculated

Risk is determined by the `v_coder_risk_analysis` database view, which combines data from `soft_skills_assessment` and `moodle_progress` for every Coder:

```sql
CASE 
  WHEN ssa.autonomy <= 2 AND mp.average_score < 70 THEN 'high'
  WHEN ssa.autonomy <= 2 OR  mp.average_score < 70 THEN 'medium'
  ELSE                                                   'low'
END AS calculated_risk_level
```

Two signals drive the calculation:

| Signal | Source | Threshold |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| `autonomy` | `soft_skills_assessment.autonomy` (1–5 scale) | ≤ 2 indicates low self-direction |
| `average_score` | `moodle_progress.average_score` | < 70 indicates academic struggle |

## The three risk levels

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Low risk">
    The Coder's autonomy score is above 2 **and** their Moodle average is 70 or higher. No immediate intervention is required. The risk badge on their card is absent or displayed in green.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Medium risk">
    Either the Coder's autonomy score is ≤ 2 **or** their Moodle average is below 70 — but not both simultaneously. This is an early warning. You should check in with the Coder and consider generating an AI report to understand what is happening.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="High risk">
    Both conditions are true: autonomy ≤ 2 **and** Moodle average < 70. The Coder is struggling with both independence and academic performance. An unresolved high-risk flag increments the **En riesgo** counter on your dashboard and triggers the red warning badge in the topbar.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Risk flags are auto-detected (`auto_detected = true`) by the platform. You can also create manual flags from a Coder's profile if you observe issues that the automated signals do not capture.
</Warning>

## The `risk_flags` table

Each flag stored in `risk_flags` represents a discrete risk event for a single Coder:

```sql
CREATE TABLE risk_flags (
  id            SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  coder_id      INT NOT NULL,
  risk_level    risk_level_enum NOT NULL,   -- 'low', 'medium', 'high'
  reason        TEXT NOT NULL,
  auto_detected BOOLEAN DEFAULT TRUE,
  detected_at   TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  resolved      BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
  resolved_at   TIMESTAMP NULL
);
```

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `risk_level` | One of `low`, `medium`, or `high` |
| `reason` | Human-readable explanation of why the flag was raised |
| `auto_detected` | `true` if the platform raised the flag; `false` if a TL created it manually |
| `resolved` | `false` while the flag is active. Set to `true` when you mark it resolved |
| `resolved_at` | Timestamp populated when the flag is resolved |

## How risk flags appear on your dashboard

Unresolved risk flags (`resolved = false`) surface in three places on the TL dashboard:

1. **Stats row** — the **En riesgo** stat card shows the total count of Coders with an active `high` or `critical` unresolved flag.
2. **Topbar badge** — a red triangle with a count appears in the top-right header when at least one high-risk Coder exists in your clan.
3. **Coders table** — each Coder's row shows a coloured risk badge. The table sorts by risk level: `critical` first, then `high`, `medium`, and unranked last.

The detail panel also shows a prominent **⚠ En riesgo** badge below the Coder's name when they have an active unresolved flag.

## Resolving a risk flag

When you have intervened and the situation is under control, mark the flag resolved:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Coder's detail panel">
    Click the Coder's row in the dashboard table to open their detail on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the active flag">
    The risk badge and reason text are visible in the detail header. Confirm you understand what triggered the flag.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark as resolved">
    Click **Resolver** in the detail panel. Kairo sets `resolved = true` and records `resolved_at = NOW()` on the flag row. The Coder's badge disappears from the table and the dashboard counters update immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  After resolving a flag, consider generating an [AI report](/features/ai-reports) for the Coder to document the situation and get recommendations for next steps.
</Tip>
