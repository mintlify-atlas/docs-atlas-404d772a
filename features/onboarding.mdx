The first time you log in to Kairo, the platform detects that your `first_login` flag is `true` and redirects you to the **Diagnóstico** screen before you can access your dashboard. This one-time assessment takes 5–8 minutes and shapes every plan Kairo generates for you.

<Info>
  You only complete the diagnostic once. You can update your profile from **Settings** at any time if your learning style changes.
</Info>

## How the diagnostic works

The assessment consists of **30 questions** organized into 7 blocks. Each block explores a distinct dimension of how you learn:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="B1 — Sensory channels">
    Do you learn better by seeing diagrams, listening, reading text, or practicing hands-on? These questions map your primary input channel.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B2 — Active vs. Reflective">
    Do you dive in immediately or prefer to think before acting? This dimension measures your processing speed preference.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B3 — Concrete vs. Intuitive">
    Are you drawn to real-world examples or abstract ideas? This determines how the AI frames explanations for you.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B4 — Sequential vs. Global">
    Do you prefer step-by-step instruction or starting with the big picture? This controls the structure of your learning plan.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B5 — Visual vs. Verbal">
    Does a diagram work better for you than a written description? This influences the format of activities in your plan.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B6 — Learning cycle">
    How do you process what you learn — by experiencing, observing, analyzing, or experimenting? Based on Kolb's model.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B7 — Integrative profile">
    Final questions that cross all previous dimensions to build your complete learning profile.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Kairo analyses your answers using three established frameworks — **VARK**, **Felder-Soloman**, and **Kolb** — to determine your learning style.

## Answering questions

Navigating the diagnostic is straightforward:

- **Click** an option to select it. The next question appears automatically.
- **Keyboard shortcuts** — press `A`, `B`, `C`, or `D` to select options without using the mouse.
- **Back button** — use the `← Atrás` button at any time to change a previous answer.

There are no right or wrong answers. Always choose your first instinct — the option that best reflects how you actually behave.

A global progress bar at the top of the screen shows your position: block label (e.g., `B1`), current question number, and overall percentage.

## What the assessment measures

The diagnostic produces scores for **5 soft skill dimensions**, each rated from 1 to 5:

| Dimension | What it captures |
|-----------|------------------|
| `autonomy` | How independently you work and self-direct your learning |
| `time_management` | How effectively you plan and prioritize your study time |
| `problem_solving` | Your approach to debugging and breaking down challenges |
| `communication` | How clearly you express technical and non-technical ideas |
| `teamwork` | How you collaborate in group settings |

It also determines your **learning style**: `visual`, `auditory`, `kinesthetic`, or `mixed`.

## How responses are stored

When you submit the diagnostic, Kairo writes your results to the `soft_skills_assessment` table:

```sql
CREATE TABLE soft_skills_assessment (
  id              SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  coder_id        INT NOT NULL UNIQUE,
  autonomy        INT NOT NULL CHECK (autonomy BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  time_management INT NOT NULL CHECK (time_management BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  problem_solving INT NOT NULL CHECK (problem_solving BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  communication   INT NOT NULL CHECK (communication BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  teamwork        INT NOT NULL CHECK (teamwork BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  learning_style  learning_style_enum NOT NULL,
  raw_answers     JSONB,
  assessed_at     TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);
```

The `raw_answers` JSONB column stores your full response sequence so the AI can reference the exact pattern of choices, not just the final scores.

## What happens after you finish

<Steps>
  <Step title="first_login flag is cleared">
    The `first_login` column on your `users` record is set to `false`, so you won't see the diagnostic again.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learning style is cached">
    Your resolved learning style (e.g., `visual`) is written to `users.learning_style_cache` for fast access by the dashboard and AI services.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan generation is triggered">
    Kairo fires a request to the Python AI microservice at `POST /generate-plan`. The service reads your soft skills snapshot, your current Moodle progress, and your module context to build a personalized 4-week study plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You land on your dashboard">
    Once the plan is ready, you are redirected to your dashboard where your soft skills ring, learning style badge, and active plan are displayed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you navigate away before the plan finishes generating, the AI Trainer page shows a **"Kairo está preparando tu plan"** state with a progress indicator. The plan continues generating in the background.
</Note>
