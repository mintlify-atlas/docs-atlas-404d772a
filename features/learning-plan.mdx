After you complete onboarding, Kairo generates a **personalized 4-week study plan** tailored to your learning profile and your current position in the bootcamp. Every plan consists of 20 daily sessions organized across 4 weeks, with activities calibrated to your soft skills scores, learning style, and any topics you're struggling with.

## What the AI uses to build your plan

Kairo's Python AI microservice (`POST /generate-plan`) pulls together three data sources before generating a single line of your plan:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Soft skills snapshot" icon="brain">
    Your 5 dimension scores (autonomy, time management, problem solving, communication, teamwork) and learning style from the onboarding diagnostic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Moodle progress" icon="graduation-cap">
    Your current week, average score, completed weeks history, and any topics where your scores indicate difficulty.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Module context" icon="book">
    The name and topic list of your current bootcamp module, so every activity is grounded in what you're studying right now.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The plan generation supports two modes:

| Mode | Trigger | Focus |
|------|---------|-------|
| `interpretive` | Default for first-time plan | Builds around your learning style and module topics |
| `analytical` | When Moodle data is available | Incorporates your average score and struggling topics to target weak areas |

## Plan structure

Every plan spans **20 days across 4 weeks** (5 working days per week). Each day contains two activity cards:

- A **technical activity** — focused on a module topic or struggling concept
- A **soft skill activity** — a short reflection or practice exercise targeting one of your 5 dimensions

### The 4-week arc

| Week | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Week 1 | Foundation — address highest-priority gaps |
| Week 2 | Reinforcement — deepen understanding of core topics |
| Week 3 | Application — practice through guided exercises |
| Week 4 | Simulation — autonomous work and performance test preparation |

Day 20 is always a **performance simulation day**: you work under timed, no-help conditions to assess your readiness.

## How plans are stored

Completed plans are persisted in the `complementary_plans` table:

```sql
CREATE TABLE complementary_plans (
  id                     SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  coder_id               INT NOT NULL,
  module_id              INT NOT NULL,
  plan_content           JSONB NOT NULL,
  priority_level         priority_level_enum DEFAULT 'medium',
  soft_skills_snapshot   JSONB,
  moodle_status_snapshot JSONB,
  targeted_soft_skill    VARCHAR,
  is_active              BOOLEAN DEFAULT TRUE,
  generated_at           TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);
```

Key columns:

- **`plan_content`** — the full AI-generated plan as JSONB, including all 20 days of activities
- **`soft_skills_snapshot`** — a point-in-time copy of your soft skills scores at generation time, used for plan comparison
- **`moodle_status_snapshot`** — stores `plan_type`, `current_week`, `module_name`, `average_score`, and `struggling_topics` as they were when the plan was created
- **`targeted_soft_skill`** — the single soft skill dimension the AI prioritized for this plan cycle
- **`is_active`** — only one plan is active at a time; generating a new plan deactivates the previous one

## Viewing your plan on the dashboard

Your dashboard displays your active plan status in the **welcome card** at the top of the screen. The plan badge appears when `is_active = true`:

```
⚡ Plan activo
```

The full plan view is available in the **AI Trainer** tab, where you can navigate week by week and day by day through all 20 sessions.

## Requesting a new plan

You can request a new plan at any time from the AI Trainer screen by clicking **"Generar mi plan personalizado"**. Kairo:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deactivates your current plan">
    The existing active plan's `is_active` flag is set to `false`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sends a fire-and-forget request to Python">
    The Node.js backend posts to `POST /generate-plan` on the Python microservice without blocking the UI response. You see a "generating" state immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Polls for completion">
    The AI Trainer page polls `GET /api/coder/plan` every few seconds. When `hasPlan: true` is returned, the new plan loads automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you refresh the page during plan generation, the AI Trainer automatically resumes polling and picks up the plan as soon as it's ready.
</Tip>

## Priority ordering of focus cards

Each plan includes 6 focus cards ordered by the `priority_level_enum`:

```sql
CREATE TYPE priority_level_enum AS ENUM ('low', 'medium', 'high');
```

The distribution is always **2 high, 2 medium, 2 low**. High-priority cards target your struggling topics and lowest-scoring soft skill dimension directly. See [Focus cards](/features/focus-cards) for a full breakdown of what each card contains.
